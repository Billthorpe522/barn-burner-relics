## 1. CONCEPT

A "Look what we've got coming at you Monday" video. Ten bannerstones photographed as a single collection under continuous LED lighting, black drape backdrop, BBR flyer in frame. Goal: drive Facebook group members to the Monday 5pm showcase AND nudge one or two collectors in the camera's direction who might have a bannerstone collection to sell.

**Frame it as:** "These aren't just pieces — they're 10 lifetimes of collecting, in one frame."

**Frame it NOT as:** A polished eBay listing. Authenticity first, story always. Show the table, show the stickers, show the work.

---

## 2. HOOK (first 15 seconds)

The thumbnail/hero shot is the opening frame. Open cold on the wide shot of all 10 pieces. Hold for 3 seconds. Then start talking.

**Suggested opener (paraphrase, not script):**

> "Ten bannerstones on the table tonight. Some pink slate, some granite, a butterfly you've already seen if you've been in the group, and a piece in the front row I haven't shown anyone yet. We're going to walk through every one of these before Monday night — and if you've got a bannerstone collection sitting in a drawer you've been thinking about selling, stay to the end. Nick's phone number's on the flyer behind them."

**Why this hook works:**
- Numbered promise ("ten pieces") — sets expectation of a complete walk-through
- Callback to a known piece (butterfly, Cuckler #3287) — establishes credibility fast
- "I haven't shown anyone yet" — creates scarcity for the new piece
- "Stay to the end" — retention hook for collectors watching
- Phones number visible in the B-roll frame the whole time — subliminal CTA

## 5. CTA (last 60 seconds)

Stand back up from the table. Frame the whole shot again. Direct address to camera.

**Suggested closer (paraphrase):**

> "Ten pieces. Monday at five Central. If you've been thinking about bidding, the group's where the action is — link in the post. And if you've got a bannerstone collection you've been sitting on, Nick's number's right there on the flyer. He buys collections. Not single pieces — collections. The whole drawer."

**Why this CTA works:**
- "The whole drawer" — frames the buyout as the easy option, not piecemeal
- Specific time ("Monday at five Central") — not vague
- Two CTAs (bidders AND sellers) — doubles the audience value of the video

---

## 6. WHAT NOT TO SAY

- **Don't quote prices on video.** Anchor prices are for Monday's showcase. Mention "starting bids" only.
- **Don't speculate on provenance.** If a sticker number is unreadable, say "we'll verify on intake" — never guess. (This is the Cuckler-3281-vs-3287 lesson.)
- **Don't reference the "test website under construction."** That framing is for the catalog (`index.html`), not for Facebook. On Facebook, BBR is the brand.
- **Don't oversell the star piece.** The shape sells itself. Let the closeup at 3:00 do the work.
